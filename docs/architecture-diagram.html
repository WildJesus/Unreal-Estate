universes/calc.ts

Affordability Engine

Pure math — takes a price, year, and region; returns a full BurdenComparison object.

7-step calculation

  • Step 1 — Estimate historical price via price index ratio
  • Step 2 — Monthly mortgage payments (current & historical rates)
  • Step 3 — Household net income (2 earners × regional wage × 77%)
  • Step 4 — Burden ratio = payment ÷ income
  • Step 5 — Stress multiplier = today's burden ÷ historical burden
  • Step 6 — Burden-equivalent price (what it "should" cost)
  • Step 7 — Equivalent monthly payment at today's rates

Also provides

  • CZK formatting (Czech locale, space-separated thousands)
  • Price parsing (handles sreality obfuscation)
  • Burden & multiplier formatting
universes/data.ts

Economic Dataset

All data hardcoded — zero external API calls. Bundled at build time.

27 years of data (2000–2026)

  • Mortgage interest rates (ČNB / Hypoindex)
  • National apartment price index (2015 = 100)
  • National average gross monthly wage (ČSÚ)

14 Czech regions

  • Regional wage anchors (2025 ČSÚ data)
  • Regional price growth multipliers (MVP estimates)
  • Historical regional data derived from 2025 ratios

Model defaults

  • 10% down payment, 30-year term
  • 2 earners per household, 77% gross-to-net

Key Business Concepts
Burden Ratio
Monthly mortgage payment ÷ household net income. The core metric. E.g., 0.55 = "55% of take-home pay goes to mortgage."
Stress Multiplier
Today's burden ÷ historical burden. E.g., 1.68× means "housing eats 68% more of your income today than it did in year X."
Burden-Equivalent Price
"What would this flat need to cost today for the mortgage burden to feel like year X?" — the headline user-facing insight.
Regional Data
Wages and price growth differ by region. Praha prices grew ~15% faster than national; Ústecký kraj ~25% slower. 14 regions tracked.
Year Selector
User picks a comparison year (2000–2025) via quick-pick pills or a range slider. Default: 2015. Changing the year re-computes all widgets live.
Anti-Scraping Handling
Sreality injects zero-width spaces (U+200B) and per-character <span> obfuscation into prices. The scanner normalizes these before parsing.
